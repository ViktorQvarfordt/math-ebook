Let $+$ be a [closed](closure) [binary operation] on a [set] $A$ with [identity element] $0$. Let $\times$ be a closed [binary operation] on $A$. The Jacobi identity is the property of $\times$ such that $$\forall a, b, c \in A \big( a \times (b \times c) + b \times (c \times a) + c \times (a \times b) = 0\big).$$

This can be seen as a weaker form of [associativity], since if $\times$ is associative then the Jacobi identity holds.

A Lie algebra is a [vector space] $\mathfrak {g}$ equipped with an [alternating] [bilinear] [function] $\mathfrak {g}\times \mathfrak {g} \to \mathfrak {g}; (x,y) \mapsto [x,y]$ called the Lie bracket that satisfies the [Jacobi identity].

A Lie group is a group $(G, m, i)$ such that $G$ is a [smooth manifold] and group multiplication $m$ and group inversion $i$ are [smooth functions](smooth function).
